Pros

  • Evolutionary from current product
  • Solver works without AI (free tier natural)
  • Students stay in familiar step-by-step flow

Cons

  • AI feels bolted on, not integrated
  • Less impressive as portfolio piece
  • Limited room for AI-driven features
B

AI-First Tutor with Embedded Solver

Conversational AI is the primary experience. The solver is a tool the AI uses behind the scenes.

Chat Interface
Solve 5(2x-3) = 25 and explain each step

5(2x-3) = 25 → 2x-3 = 5

Wait, why can't I just expand the parentheses?

Pros

  • Most natural tutoring experience
  • AI handles any math topic, not just linear eq.
  • Impressive portfolio: real AI product

Cons

  • Free tier is weak (no AI = no product)
  • Loses the interactive step-by-step UX
  • Just another ChatGPT wrapper?
C

Math Workspace with AI Co-Pilot

A dashboard with multiple tools. Solver, AI chat, practice mode, and notebooks — each is a "workspace" the student can switch between. AI is woven through all of them.

Pros

  • Best portfolio impact — shows product thinking
  • Free tier is strong (solver + limited practice)
  • Natural place for every future feature
  • AI enhances each tool contextually

Cons

  • Largest build scope
  • Risk of shallow features if spread too thin
  • Dashboard UX can feel generic

My Recommendation: C (Math Workspace)

For a portfolio piece, C gives you the most to show. The key is phasing: MVP ships with just the Solver tab + AI co-pilot. Practice and Notebook tabs come in later phases. The workspace shell is lightweight to build but makes the product feel like a platform, not a single-feature tool. The free tier stays strong (solver works without AI), and every future feature has a natural home.
